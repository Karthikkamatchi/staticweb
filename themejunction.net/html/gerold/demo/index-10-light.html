KALAM AWARDS

INTRODUCTION

AgroEcoLink is a smart, eco-friendly farming model that integrates solar power, IoT sensors, and biogas technology into a circular farming cycle. In this system, crop waste feeds a biogas plant, generating renewable energy and nutrient-rich slurry.

The slurry returns to the soil as organic fertilizer, creating a zero-waste loop. A solar-powered Wireless Sensor Network (WSN) tracks soil, crop health, and weather in real time. Solar UV traps help manage pests without chemicals. A key feature is the mobile app “UZHAVAR THOZHAN.”

It allows live monitoring of crop health, soil status, biogas pressure, methane content, and predicts the runtime of the biogas system. The app also aids in smart irrigation management. This model boosts crop yield, cuts costs, and assists farmers in moving toward sustainable, climate-friendly agriculture.

INTRODUCTION

Now a days, farmers struggle with rising costs, reliance on chemicals, and climate issues. AgroEcoLink tackles these challenges by using renewable energy, IoT, and circular farming practices. Crop residues become energy and fertilizer instead of waste. Farmers benefit from UZHAVAR THOZHAN, a straightforward mobile app that simplifies smart farming and management.

  • Use solar-powered IoT and WSN to monitor soil, crop health, and weather in real time.
  • Develop UZHAVAR THOZHAN, a user-friendly app for tracking crops, soil, and biogas plant performance.
  • Provide pest control without chemicals using solar UV traps.
  • Optimize the biogas plant for greater energy output and organic slurry production.
  • Create a circular farming cycle that reuses waste and enriches soil fertility.
